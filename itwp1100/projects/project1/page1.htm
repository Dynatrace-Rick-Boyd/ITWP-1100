Beekeeping

People, called beekeepers, maintain colonies of honey bees for the purpose of harvesting honey, beeswax, and other byproducts of the colony. Beekeeping goes back to ancient Egypt, and is practiced on all 6 populous continents.

Beekeeping farms, called "apiaries," range from very small, independent operations to very large industrial ones.
